Latest Updates on Development at Gurgaon/Gurugram Railway Station and the Gurugram Metro

This draft summarises the latest publicly reported updates (from multiple recent articles) on the new Gurugram Metro project and related works that affect the Gurugram Railway Station area. I’ve kept the language simple so high‑school and early undergraduate students can follow the facts, figures and timelines.

Quick overview: what is happening?

Gurugram (Gurgaon) is getting a new elevated metro corridor that will connect HUDA (Millennium City Centre) to Cyber City via Old Gurugram. The corridor is about 28.5 km long in total (main line ~26.65 km plus a spur to Dwarka Expressway ~1.85 km). The project is being developed by Gurugram Metro Rail Limited (GMRL), a joint venture of the Central Government and the Government of Haryana. The project promises better connectivity, large ridership, and real estate impact in Old Gurugram.

Key features and headline numbers

  • Total corridor length: ~28.5 km (main line ~26.65 km + Dwarka Expressway spur 1.85 km).
  • Number of stations on the new line: 27 (including 1 depot).
  • Project type: Elevated corridor; signalling planned with CBTC (Communication‑Based Train Control).
  • Estimated construction cost: various figures reported – one report lists Rs 6,800 crore for the full new line; another breakdown lists centre investing ~Rs 896.19 crore and Haryana ~Rs 4,556.53 crore (total Rs 5,452.72 crore).
  • Ownership / execution: Gurugram Metro Rail Limited (GMRL). HMRTC is mentioned for broader transport planning.
  • Expected ridership projection: ~5.4 lakh by 2026 and ~7.26 lakh by 2031 (one article), and the corridor is said to benefit over 2.5 million people on a broader scale.

All 27 approved stations (short list)

The approved corridor will have 27 stations. Important named stations include:

  • Millennium City Centre (HUDA City Centre) — interchange with Delhi Metro Yellow Line
  • Sector 45, Cyber Park (Sector 46), Sector 47, Subhash Chowk (proposed interchange with future Bhondsi line), Sector 48, Sector 72A, Hero Honda Chowk
  • Udyog Vihar Phase 6, Sector 10 (near Bus Terminal), Sector 37, Basai Village (junction for Dwarka Expressway spur)
  • Sector 9, Sector 7, Sector 4 (near Gurgaon Railway Station), Sector 5, Ashok Vihar, Sector 3, Bajghera Road
  • Palam Vihar Extension, Palam Vihar, Sector 23A, Sector 22, Udyog Vihar Phase 4 & 5
  • Cyber City (interchange with Rapid Metro), Dwarka Expressway (Sector 101) — on the 1.85 km spur

Phases, contractors and timelines (multiple reports)

Reports describe the project in three phases:

  • Phase 1: Millennium City Centre (HUDA City Centre) to Sector 9 — variously described as 15 km / 15.2 km. One report said Phase 1 will have 15 stations and span 15.2 km; another report lists Phase 1 as 15.2 km with 14 stations. The tender for Phase 1 was expected to be floated by the end of July 2025 (estimated cost Rs 1,286 crore in that report).
  • A civil contract for Phase 1 (Millennium City Centre to Sector 9) was reportedly won by a joint venture of Dilip Buildcon Ltd (DBL) and RBL as the lowest bidder. Construction was anticipated to begin by September 2025. Reported cost for this contract is INR 1,503 crore and the work is targeted to be completed within 30 months from start.
  • Phase 2: Sector 9 to Cyber City — about 13–16 km depending on the report; tenders and geotechnical surveys being floated. This phase may include the Sector 5 spur to the Railway Station (see below).
  • Phase 3: development of a metro depot in Sector 33.

Important dates and progress notes from the reports:

  • Bhoomi pujan / foundation stone: reported on 3 September 2025 (one report) and 5 September 2025 (another report) with leaders present including Haryana Chief Minister Nayab Singh Saini and Union Minister Manohar Lal Khattar.
  • Site works: small works and piling have started; contractor began piling near the Sector 31 traffic signal and a casting yard beside Hero Honda Chowk was being prepared.
  • Planned schedule in one report: site work end‑2025; major construction 2026–2027; testing 2028; public operations around 2029. Other reports target completion of Phase 1 within 30 months from start (if started Sep 2025, that aligns with mid‑2027 for Phase 1 structures).

Sectors 5 → Gurugram Railway Station spur (important for the station)

There is a separate proposed 1.8 km metro spur to directly connect the upcoming Sector 5 metro station to Gurugram Railway Station. Key facts:

  • Length: 1.8 km (reported).
  • Estimated cost: Rs 450 crore.
  • Status: DPR/study by RITES completed; proposal finalised and to be sent to GMRL board, then Haryana government and Union Ministry for approval; planned to be included in Phase 2 tender once approved.
  • Land needs: the station entry/exit at the railway station will need ~1,069 sq metres (419 sq m government land, 446 sq m private land, 204 sq m railway land). The Sector 5 station area needs ~605 sq m more.
  • Earlier plan was a skywalk with escalators; state decided a dedicated metro spur is preferable to boost ridership and connectivity.
  • A separate HMRTC DPR for a Bhondsi–Gurugram railway station line is being prepared, but with this spur the Bhondsi line is likely to terminate at Sector 5 (interchange).

Construction setup and technology

Construction preparations include a three‑hectare casting yard producing precast U‑girders, pier caps and viaduct segments. Load testing on piles is underway and once cleared, full piling will begin. The contractor planned on-site facilities for over 1,200 workers in two shifts to enable near‑round‑the‑clock construction. Signalling will use CBTC for safety and precise train control.

Land acquisition policy (to speed things up)

Haryana approved a new land acquisition policy specifically for the Gurugram Metro expansion. Main points:

  • Focus on direct purchase (negotiated settlement) — avoids forced acquisition.
  • GMRL authorised to buy private land; an 11‑member Land Acquisition Committee chaired by the Gurugram Deputy Commissioner will oversee negotiations.
  • Purchase price will be consolidated and include compensation and rehabilitation benefits (aligned with RFCTLARR Act schedules). No extra R&R payments beyond the negotiated price for titleholders; non‑titleholders will be handled separately.
  • Eight‑step internal process: site inspection → committee review → public notice → negotiation & purchase → transfer, etc.

Other network expansions mentioned

  • Ballabgarh to Palwal Metro — techno‑feasibility underway; ~25 km, ~10 stations.
  • Vatika Chowk to Pachgaon Metro — DPR underway; ~30 km.
  • Sector 45 (Gurgaon) to Bata Chowk (Faridabad) — ~31 km corridor planned.
  • Millennium City Centre to Gurgaon Railway Station — another route mentioned at ~11.15 km passing Rajiv Chowk, Subhash Chowk and New Colony Mor.
  • Sector 56 to Pachgaon — new 35.2 km line with 28 stations; estimated cost ~Rs 8,500 crore; Haryana to fund most and seek a 10% central grant. This will link to RRTS at Kherki Daula and Pachgaon.

Station redevelopment at Gurugram Railway Station (separate EPC project)

A station redevelopment project is reported (EPC), including a Main Station Building (height 41.3 m), air concourse, through roof, foot over bridge, multi‑level car parking (24.8 m), platform refurbishment, sewage & water treatment, rainwater harvesting and structural glazing. The project claims an IGBC Platinum rating target and sustainable features to improve passenger experience.

Real‑world effects: connectivity and property

Officials expect the corridor to connect New and Old Gurugram, improving mobility in Old Gurugram and boosting economic and real estate activity. Reported property impacts and price ranges:

  • Sector 4: Buying Rs 4,600 to Rs 7,600 / sq ft; Renting Rs 9 to Rs 15 / sq ft.
  • Palam Vihar: Buying Rs 7,700 to Rs 12,000 / sq ft; Renting Rs 13 to Rs 21 / sq ft.
  • Sector 9: Buying Rs 5,993 to Rs 6,351 / sq ft (builder floor typical).
  • Ashok Vihar: Buying Rs 6,027 to Rs 6,112 / sq ft.

Other practical notes

Reports say Gurugram Metro will allow travel with Delhi Metro cards (interoperability). The new line will interchange with the existing Delhi Metro Yellow Line at HUDA City Centre and with Rapid Metro at Cyber City. One article also described Gurugram Metro as going to be the first privately owned and operated metro project in India (reported claim).

Summary (plainly)

In short: the Gurugram Metro project is now formally approved and work has started. The main new corridor is ~28.5 km with 27 stations linking HUDA City Centre to Cyber City via Old Gurugram, plus a small Dwarka Expressway spur. A separate but important proposed 1.8 km, Rs 450 crore spur would directly link Sector 5 to Gurugram Railway Station and requires small parcels of land. Construction is underway in parts (casting yard, piling), contracts have been awarded for Phase 1, and multiple timelines and cost figures have been reported — so expect updates and official clarifications as work progresses.
